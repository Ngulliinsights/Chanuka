  • Dr. Elizabeth Wambui

    Official Expert Health Policy Director, Ministry of Health

    The most significant aspect of this bill is how it addresses healthcare deserts in rural counties. Our research shows that 63% of rural Kenyans must travel more than 5km to reach basic healthcare services. The proposed community health centers would reduce this distance to under 2km for over 70% of the rural population.

  • Prof. James Ochieng

    Official Expert Health Economics Researcher, University of Nairobi

    While the bill's ambitions are admirable, there are significant funding challenges. The proposed expansion of NHIF coverage would require a 45% increase in the current healthcare budget. Without clear revenue sources, this could strain public finances or result in reduced benefits per person. I recommend phased implementation with targeted coverage expansion starting with vulnerable populations.
