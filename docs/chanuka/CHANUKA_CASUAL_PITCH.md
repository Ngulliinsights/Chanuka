# Chanuka — The Casual Pitch

## What is it?

Chanuka is a civic accountability platform that makes it impossible for Kenyan MPs to hide from their own voting record. Think of it as "GitHub for democracy, with electoral consequences" — you can track bills, see exactly who voted for what, understand what it means for your life, and generate the kind of ward-level pressure that actually moves politicians.

## The Real Problem

Right now, if you want to know what's happening in Parliament, you're stuck reading dense legal documents, hunting through government websites, or relying on media that has every incentive to keep things vague. Most people have no idea what bills are being passed or how they'll affect their lives until it's too late.

But here's the deeper problem nobody talks about: **information alone doesn't change behavior.** Politicians across the world have learned to absorb transparency tools — to let civic outrage cycle through a comment thread and disappear. Knowing what an MP did means nothing if there's no mechanism to make them pay for it.

Chanuka is built around that gap. We don't just inform. We generate political cost.

## What We Built

A platform where civic knowledge converts into electoral accountability:

- **Real-time bill tracking** — follow legislation like a GitHub repo, from introduction to presidential assent
- **AI-powered analysis** — complex legislation explained in plain language, with constitutional implications automatically flagged
- **Electoral accountability engine** — every MP's voting record mapped to their constituency, timestamped, and tied to their next election cycle
- **Ward-level sentiment reporting** — community votes aggregated by constituency and delivered in formats that campaign managers and civil society advocates can actually use
- **Legal brief generation** — constitutional violations don't just get flagged, they get packaged for petition filing at the Constitutional Court
- **Real-time notifications** — know when bills you care about move forward, before it's too late to act
- **English and Swahili** — because democracy shouldn't have a language barrier

## The Critical Distinction

Most civic platforms measure **engagement** — users, sessions, comments. We measure **outcomes** — MPs who changed votes under constituency pressure, bills challenged successfully in court, candidates who lost seats after voting records became local campaign material.

Engagement without consequence is just a well-designed archive of impunity.

## The Tech

Professional monorepo — React + Express + PostgreSQL, fully TypeScript, properly tested, and built to scale. Real-time notifications, ML-powered analysis, and WCAG AA accessibility so no one is locked out.

## Current Status

Pre-launch, targeting Q2 2026. Core features are working — bill tracking, user auth, community features, constitutional analysis. We're refining accessibility, resolving TypeScript errors, and building out the electoral accountability layer as a primary feature, not an afterthought.

## Why It Matters

The global pattern is clear: transparency without consequence is theater. Leaders have learned they can survive scrutiny. Chanuka is built on the premise that what politicians actually respond to is quantified, localized, electoral risk — and we're building the machine that produces it.
