# NED Grant Application — Letters of Support Templates
## Three Templates for Three Partner Types

*Instructions for use: Send each organisation a customised version of the relevant template. Ask them to print on their letterhead, sign, and return as PDF. Give each organisation at least three weeks. Follow up at day ten if no response. The letter is only valuable if it is specific — ask each organisation to fill in the bracketed sections with their own programme details before signing.*

**RE: Letter of Support and Partnership Commitment — Chanuka Constitutional Violation Detection Layer**

Dear NED Africa Programme Team,

[Organisation name] works at the intersection of constitutional rights and civic access — ensuring that Kenya's constitution functions as a living document that protects citizens' rights, not merely a formal text that informs legal proceedings.

We write in support of Chanuka's NED grant application, and specifically to endorse and commit to supporting the platform's constitutional violation detection feature — a capability that directly extends our constitutional accountability mandate into the parliamentary monitoring space.

Kenya's legislative history includes multiple instances of bills that contained provisions conflicting with constitutional protections — some introduced deliberately, some through legislative oversight, all with material consequences for citizens' rights. The Finance Bill 2023 is the most prominent recent example, but it is not exceptional. The pattern is structural: omnibus legislation routinely embeds consequential provisions that receive no separate public scrutiny before passage.

Chanuka's constitutional violation detection layer addresses this structural vulnerability through automated NLP analysis that flags potential conflicts between bill provisions and constitutional protections, surfacing those flags for citizens in plain language before legislation passes. This is the proactive constitutional accountability function that our organisation performs reactively — through litigation, legal opinion, and public advocacy — after legislation has already passed.

We have agreed to the following partnership with Chanuka during the NED grant period:

- [Specific commitment: Legal review of the constitutional flagging methodology / Advisory input on constitutional risk categories / Co-authorship of plain-language constitutional guides for the top 20 most commonly misunderstood constitutional provisions / Joint public communications on flagged legislation]

- Access to [organisation name]'s constitutional analysis database for training the NLP detection model, under a data sharing agreement that maintains the database's integrity and attribution.

- Staff time: [specific person, title] will serve on Chanuka's constitutional methodology advisory group during the grant period, providing [X hours per month] of expert input.

This partnership converts Chanuka's technological capability into constitutionally grounded analysis — ensuring that the violation detection layer reflects rigorous legal reasoning, not automated pattern-matching without expert validation.

We endorse Chanuka's NED application and confirm the partnership commitments described above.

We write in support of Chanuka's NED grant application because the platform addresses the specific gap our civic engagement work consistently encounters: citizens who are motivated to hold their representatives accountable but who lack accessible, verified information about what those representatives have actually done.

Our community members know their MPs. They have attended their rallies, received their harambee contributions, and voted for or against them. What they have not been able to access — in a format they can use and share — is a verified record of how those MPs voted on the legislation that directly affects their lives: the bills that determined their fuel costs, their school fees, their market licensing requirements.

Chanuka's constituency-level MP accountability scorecard is specifically the tool our community mobilisation work has lacked. A shareable, verified, plain-language accountability profile for each Nairobi MP — available on both smartphones and basic handsets through the USSD interface — is the resource our community leaders have asked for repeatedly and that no existing platform currently provides.
