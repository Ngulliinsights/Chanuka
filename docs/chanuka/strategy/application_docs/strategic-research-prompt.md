# Strategic Intelligence Research Prompt
## For: Civic Technology / Democratic Accountability / East Africa Context
## Use with: Deep Research, Perplexity, Claude Research Mode, or manual research workflow

---

## CONTEXT TO PROVIDE BEFORE RUNNING THIS PROMPT

Before executing this research prompt, provide the following context to the research tool:

> "I am the founder of Chanuka, an open-source parliamentary accountability platform for Kenya's National Assembly. The platform converts MP voting records into constituency-level accountability scorecards, includes a plain-language bill translation engine, and a constitutional violation detection layer. The platform is operational with a pilot covering 17 Nairobi constituencies and 4.3 million constituents, timed to Kenya's 2027 election cycle. I am building a strategic picture of the fellowship, grant, and employment landscape to identify high-fit opportunities and prepare competitive applications."

### BLOCK B: LANDSCAPE INTELLIGENCE

**B1. The Post-USAID Funding Vacuum**

Research what has actually happened to the democratic governance and civil society funding landscape in East Africa following the USAID dismantlement in 2025. Specifically:
- Which funders have explicitly announced increased funding for East Africa civil society to fill the USAID gap?
- Which civil society organisations in Kenya have lost USAID funding and are now seeking alternative partnerships or programme integration?
- What is the estimated total value of USAID funding lost to Kenya civil society, and what fraction has been replaced?
- Which donors have been publicly critical of the USAID withdrawal and positioned themselves as alternatives?
- Are there any new pooled funds or emergency mechanisms specifically for East Africa civil society accountability work?

**B2. Kenya Civic Technology Landscape — Current State**

Research the current state of civic technology in Kenya as of 2025–2026:
- Who are the currently active organisations doing parliamentary monitoring, legislative tracking, or MP accountability work in Kenya?
- What tools exist (list specific platforms, not just organisations) and what gap does each leave that Chanuka addresses?
- Which Kenyan civil society organisations have received international funding in 2024–2025 for governance or civic technology work? (Return organisation name, funder, amount if public, and programme focus)
- What is the current status of Mzalendo Trust — funding, staffing, active programmes?
- Are there any organisations that have attempted MP accountability scoring in Kenya previously? What happened to those efforts?

**B3. 2027 Kenya Election Cycle — Donor Intelligence**

Research what international donors and civil society funders are planning around Kenya's 2027 election cycle:
- Which funders have announced or are known to be planning election-related programmes for Kenya 2026–2027?
- What are the stated priority areas for election support (voter education, election observation, civic tech, etc.)?
- Which organisations have received or are likely to receive election-cycle funding, and for what programmes?
- What is the timeline for election-related grant applications — when are funders typically making commitments for 2027 election programming?

### BLOCK C: PERSON-SPECIFIC INTELLIGENCE

**C1. Fellowship Alumni Network Mapping**

For the following fellowships, identify 5–10 recent alumni (2019–2025) whose work is most similar to Chanuka — civic technology, parliamentary accountability, open data, democratic governance in Africa. For each alumnus: name, country, fellowship year, project description, current role, and whether they are publicly reachable (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, organisational website).

Fellowships: Shuttleworth, Mozilla, Ashoka, Omidyar, TED, Echoing Green

Goal: Identify warm introduction pathways and understand what successful fellowship applications in this space look like from the inside.

**C2. Key Decision-Maker Mapping**

Research and identify the specific individuals — by name and role — currently responsible for the following decisions:

- NED Africa programme: Who is the programme officer covering Kenya civil society? What is the best contact route for a pre-submission conversation?
- Code for Africa Kenya: Who is the current Kenya Country Director or most senior Kenya-based staff? What is their background and public profile?
- Shuttleworth Foundation: Who is currently on the selection panel, and who are the most recent fellows whose work overlaps with civic technology?
- Luminate Group: Who manages the civic empowerment portfolio for Africa? Have they made any recent public statements about priorities?
- Mozilla Foundation: Who manages the Responsible Tech fellowship for the Africa/Global South track?

**C3. Comparative Founder Intelligence**

Research 5–10 founders or leaders of comparable civic technology platforms in Africa who have successfully secured major fellowships or grants. For each: platform name, country, founding year, what problem it addresses, which fellowships or grants they have secured, and any public statements about their application strategy or what differentiated their application.

Comparable platforms to research: Odekro (Ghana), Ma3Route (Kenya), BudgIT (Nigeria), Oulanyah Monitor (Uganda), PesaCheck (Kenya/regional), OpenUp (South Africa), Grassroot (South Africa), Democracy Works (South Africa)

### BLOCK D: STRATEGIC TIMING INTELLIGENCE

**D1. Application Deadline Calendar — Next 18 Months**

Compile a complete calendar of application deadlines for all opportunities identified in Block A, organised by deadline date. Format as a table with columns: Deadline, Opportunity Name, Type (Fellowship/Grant/Job), Amount/Value, Notes on fit with Chanuka profile.

**D2. Sequencing Intelligence**

Based on the landscape research above, answer the following strategic questions:
- Which opportunities close soonest and should be prioritised immediately regardless of fit ranking?
- Which opportunities explicitly require prior recognition (another fellowship, existing grant, published research) as a prerequisite — and therefore should be sequenced later?
- Which funders have co-funded the same organisations or projects, suggesting they operate as a cluster where one success accelerates others?
- Which opportunities would most strengthen a subsequent Shuttleworth application specifically?

**D3. The Warm Introduction Map**

Based on all research above, identify the three highest-value warm introduction pathways — where a single connection to a named individual would most accelerate access to the highest-value opportunities. For each: the named individual, their institutional role, why they are the highest-leverage connection, and the most likely pathway to a warm introduction (mutual connection, conference, public engagement, etc.).

## OUTPUT FORMAT REQUESTED

Return findings in the following structure:

1. **Immediate Action Items** (opportunities closing within 90 days, ranked by fit)
2. **Fellowship Intelligence** (findings from A1, structured by fellowship)
3. **Grant Intelligence** (findings from A2, structured by funder)
4. **Employment Intelligence** (findings from A3, structured by organisation)
5. **Landscape Intelligence** (findings from B1–B3, narrative format)
6. **Person-Specific Intelligence** (findings from C1–C3, structured by category)
7. **Strategic Calendar** (findings from D1, table format)
8. **Sequencing Recommendations** (findings from D2–D3, prioritised list)

Flag any finding that is uncertain, outdated, or requires verification before acting on it.

---

## VERIFICATION LAYER

After completing the research, run the following verification checks:

- For every deadline listed: confirm the source URL and the date it was last updated. Fellowship deadlines change. Do not report a deadline without a verifiable source.
- For every named individual: confirm current role. People move. A programme officer who left 8 months ago is not useful contact intelligence.
- For every grant amount listed: confirm whether it is per-project, per-year, or total programme — grant amounts are frequently misreported.
- For every "open" opportunity: confirm the current cycle status. Many fellowship websites list past cycles without clearly marking them as closed.
