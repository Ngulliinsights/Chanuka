Chanuka employs a systematic, multi-layered approach to analyze potential conflicts of interest and external influences on legislation. Our methodology combines quantitative data analysis with qualitative assessment to provide transparent, evidence-based evaluations of legislative integrity.

Five-Stage Analysis Process

Each legislative proposal undergoes rigorous examination through our structured five-stage process, ensuring comprehensive coverage of potential conflicts while maintaining analytical objectivity. This systematic approach allows us to identify patterns and connections that might otherwise remain hidden from public view.

1
Official Records Foundation

Systematic compilation of verifiable government data including financial disclosures, committee assignments, and voting histories to establish documented baseline connections.

2
Financial Influence Mapping

Advanced tracking of campaign contributions and financial relationships across multiple election cycles to identify potential monetary influences on policy positions.

3
Content Origin Analysis

Natural language processing comparison of bill text with industry publications and lobbying materials to detect external authorship indicators.

4
Intelligence Integration

Incorporation of verified open source intelligence from credible media, academic research, and civil society investigations to provide crucial context.

5
Synthesis & Scoring

Proprietary algorithm combines all data points to generate nuanced conflict risk scores, factoring connection strength and contextual relevance.

Technical Analysis Methods

Each analytical method serves a specific purpose in building a comprehensive picture of potential legislative conflicts, and the combination of these approaches provides robust detection capabilities that individual methods could not achieve alone.

Advanced Natural Language Processing

Our NLP systems employ transformer-based language models trained specifically on legislative and policy documents to identify semantic similarities that extend beyond simple word matching. The system compares bill text against databases containing over 2.3 million industry documents, lobbying materials, think tank publications, and previous legislative proposals. This analysis can detect when legislative language has been adapted from external sources even when surface-level paraphrasing attempts to obscure the relationship.

Temporal Pattern Recognition

Time-series analysis algorithms examine the temporal relationships between financial transactions, policy announcements, legislative introductions, and voting behaviors. The system establishes baseline patterns for each legislator and identifies statistically significant deviations that may indicate external influence. Machine learning models trained on historical data can predict the probability that observed timing correlations occurred by chance versus deliberate coordination.

Multi-Layer Network Analysis

Graph neural networks map complex webs of relationships connecting legislators, corporations, lobbying firms, campaign contributors, and policy beneficiaries. The system identifies indirect influence pathways that may span multiple degrees of separation, revealing hidden connections that traditional analysis might miss. Network centrality algorithms highlight entities that serve as crucial bridges in influence networks, even when their direct involvement appears minimal.

Unsupervised learning algorithms establish normal behavioral baselines for individual legislators based on their historical voting patterns, campaign finance activities, and policy positions. The system then identifies significant deviations from these established patterns, particularly when such changes correlate with external events like major contributions or lobbying contacts. Ensemble methods combine multiple detection approaches to minimize false positives while maintaining high sensitivity to genuine anomalies.
