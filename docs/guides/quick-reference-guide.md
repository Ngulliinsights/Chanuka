# Quick Reference: Kenyan Legal Research Resources

## Essential Websites

### Primary Legal Sources

**Kenya Law (Official Law Reports)**
- URL: http://kenyalaw.org
- Content: Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, High Court decisions
- Search: http://kenyalaw.org/caselaw/
- Download: Free PDF downloads of judgments
- Note: Most comprehensive source for constitutional cases

**Parliament of Kenya**
- URL: http://www.parliament.go.ke
- Bills: http://www.parliament.go.ke/the-national-assembly/house-business/bills
- Hansard: http://www.parliament.go.ke/the-national-assembly/house-business/hansard
- Committees: Committee reports and proceedings

**Kenya Gazette**
- URL: http://kenyalaw.org/kenya_gazette/
- Content: Executive orders, regulations, appointments
- Published: Weekly
- Critical for: Tracking workarounds and implementation

**Constitution of Kenya 2010**
- Full text: http://www.kenyalaw.org/lex/actview.xql?actid=Const2010
- Chapter-by-chapter access
- Includes all amendments

### Government Portals

**eCitizen**
- URL: https://www.ecitizen.go.ke
- Use: Company registry searches
- Cost: Small fees for searches

**Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC)**
- URL: https://www.eacc.go.ke
- Reports: Annual reports, investigation outcomes
- Disclosures: Public officer asset declarations (limited)

**Office of the Auditor General**
- URL: https://www.oagkenya.go.ke
- Reports: National and county audit reports
- Critical for: Financial transparency research

## Priority Research Targets

### Top 20 Constitutional Cases (START HERE)

#### Supreme Court (Binding Precedents)

1. **Speaker of the Senate v Attorney General [2013]**
   - Topic: Devolution, revenue allocation
   - Articles: 218, 96
   - Why: Foundational devolution case
   - URL: http://kenyalaw.org/caselaw/cases/view/89796/

2. **Coalition for Reform and Democracy (CORD) v Republic [2015]**
   - Topic: Public participation
   - Articles: 10, 118, 232, 259
   - Why: Defines public participation requirements
   - URL: http://kenyalaw.org/caselaw/cases/view/104318/

3. **Trusted Society of Human Rights Alliance v Attorney General [2012]**
   - Topic: Judicial review powers
   - Articles: 159, 165
   - Why: Establishes scope of judicial oversight
   - URL: http://kenyalaw.org/caselaw/cases/view/83217/

4. **Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission v National Super Alliance [2017]**
   - Topic: Electoral process, presidential elections
   - Articles: 81, 86, 138
   - Why: Historic election nullification
   - URL: http://kenyalaw.org/caselaw/cases/view/141689/

5. **Law Society of Kenya v Attorney General (BBI Appeal) [2022]**
   - Topic: Constitutional amendment process
   - Articles: 255-257
   - Why: Latest precedent on amendment procedure
   - URL: http://kenyalaw.org/caselaw/cases/view/229324/

#### Court of Appeal (Persuasive Authority)

6. **Kenya National Commission on Human Rights v Attorney General [2018]**
   - Topic: Judicial vetting
   - Articles: 165, 166, 172
   - Why: Judicial independence issues

7. **Anarita Karimi Njeru v Republic [2016]**
   - Topic: Right to information
   - Articles: 35, 252
   - Why: Access to information scope

8. **Institute of Social Accountability v National Assembly [2015]**
   - Topic: Parliamentary process, public participation
   - Articles: 118, 255
   - Why: Participation in legislation

#### High Court (Recent Challenges)

9. **Kituo Cha Sheria v Attorney General [2017]**
   - Topic: Economic and social rights
   - Articles: 43, 19-26
   - Why: Housing rights, forced evictions

10. **Okiya Omtatah Okoiti v Cabinet Secretary, Ministry of Finance [2020]**
    - Topic: Public debt, financial management
    - Articles: 201, 210, 231
    - Why: Debt ceiling, sovereign guarantees

11. **Law Society of Kenya v Attorney General (NIIMS) [2020]**
    - Topic: Data protection, privacy
    - Articles: 31, 32
    - Why: Digital rights, government databases

12. **Katiba Institute v Cabinet Secretary Ministry of Education [2021]**
    - Topic: Competency-Based Curriculum
    - Articles: 43, 118, 259
    - Why: Public participation in education policy

13. **Owners of Motor Vessel 'Lillian S' v Caltex Oil Kenya Ltd [1989]**
    - Topic: Fundamental rights interpretation
    - Why: Pre-2010 jurisprudence still influential

14. **Mumo Matemu v Trusted Society of Human Rights Alliance [2013]**
    - Topic: Judicial independence
    - Articles: 160, 165
    - Why: Appointment of Chief Justice

15. **Speaker of the National Assembly v Judges and Magistrates Vetting Board [2013]**
    - Topic: Transitional provisions
    - Sixth Schedule
    - Why: Implementation of new constitution

16. **Samuel Kamau Macharia & Another v Kenya Commercial Bank Ltd [2012]**
    - Topic: Fair hearing, access to justice
    - Articles: 48, 50
    - Why: Due process requirements

17. **Patricia Asero Ochieng & 2 Others v Attorney General [2012]**
    - Topic: Children's rights
    - Articles: 53, 54, 55
    - Why: Best interests of child principle

18. **Commission on Administrative Justice v Attorney General [2019]**
    - Topic: Administrative justice
    - Article 47
    - Why: Fair administrative action

19. **Coalition for Reform and Democracy (CORD) v Republic of Kenya [2015]**
    - Topic: Security Laws Amendment Act
    - Articles: 24, 33, 36, 37
    - Why: Balancing security and rights

20. **Centre for Rights Education and Awareness (CREA) v Cabinet Secretary [2020]**
    - Topic: Gender equality, women's rights
    - Articles: 27, 43, 45
    - Why: Affirmative action implementation

## Known Implementation Workarounds (START RESEARCHING)

### Category 1: High Priority (Clear Evidence)

**BBI Constitutional Amendment Attempt**
- Original: Building Bridges Initiative Bill, 2020
- Failed: Supreme Court, 2022
- Potential workarounds to research:
  - County revenue allocation changes via executive
  - IEBC reconstitution attempts
  - Judiciary Fund changes
  - Women representation initiatives
- Start with: Media archives May 2022 - Present

**Housing Levy**
- Original: Various housing bills with levy provisions
- Failed: Multiple parliamentary attempts
- Workaround: Implemented via Kenya Kwanza Affordable Housing Act, 2024
- Research: Regulation vs legislation approach
- Sources: Kenya Gazette, Treasury Circulars

**Huduma Namba / NIIMS**
- Context: Data collection without comprehensive legislation
- Issues: Privacy concerns, statutory gaps
- Workaround: Administrative vs statutory framework
- Court cases: LSK v AG [2020] - challenge to system
- Research: Executive implementation vs legislative approval

### Category 2: Medium Priority (Suspected Cases)

**Police Reforms**
- Failed bills: Various police reform bills 2013-2020
- Potential workarounds: Administrative restructuring, presidential directives
- Research: Compare failed bills to actual reforms implemented

**IEBC Reconstitution**
- Context: Attempts to change IEBC outside legislative process
- Failed: Selection Panel Act challenges
- Workarounds: Administrative attempts, executive pressure
- Research: 2016-2017 period, 2022-2023 period

**County Revenue Allocation**
- Context: Parliamentary deadlocks on formula
- Workarounds: Presidential mediation, executive coordination
- Research: Annual Division of Revenue processes 2013-present

**Betting and Gaming Regulation**
- Failed: Comprehensive gambling bills
- Workarounds: Tax measures, licensing changes
- Research: 2019-2020 gambling regulation changes

### Category 3: Historical Cases (Learning Purposes)

**Nubian Rights**
- Context: Citizenship and land rights
- Resolution: Court-driven vs legislative
- Research: How judicial decisions created policy change

**Forest Conservation**
- Failed: Various forest bills
- Workarounds: Executive actions on forest land
- Notable: Mau Forest evictions, Karura Forest protection

## Conflict of Interest Research Starting Points

### High-Profile Cases (Public Record)

**Known Cases to Document:**

1. **Gaming Industry & Legislators**
   - Multiple MPs with stakes in betting companies
   - Bills affecting gambling regulation
   - Sources: EACC disclosures, investigative reports

2. **Real Estate & Land Bills**
   - Legislators owning large land parcels
   - Bills on land use, zoning, valuation
   - Sources: Land registry, public disclosures

3. **Agriculture & Food Security**
   - MPs with agribusiness interests
   - Bills on agricultural subsidies, regulations
   - Sources: Company registry, ministry records

4. **Financial Services**
   - Legislators with banking, insurance stakes
   - Bills regulating financial sector
   - Sources: EACC, Capital Markets Authority

### Research Approach:

**Step 1:** Identify high-value bills (e.g., gaming, real estate, finance)
**Step 2:** Research sponsors and key supporters
**Step 3:** Cross-reference with:
   - EACC asset declarations
   - Company registry (eCitizen)
   - Business media coverage
   - Civil society reports

## Research Tips

**For Court Cases:**
1. Always download full judgment PDF
2. Read headnotes first for quick summary
3. Focus on "held" section for legal principle
4. Note dissenting opinions (show controversy)
5. Check if case has been cited in later judgments

**For Constitutional Provisions:**
1. Read full article text first
2. Check which bills have cited this provision
3. Search Kenya Law for cases applying provision
4. Note any amendments since 2010

**For Workarounds:**
1. Start with failed bill
2. Search Kenya Gazette for date 3-12 months after
3. Look for executive orders, regulations in same subject area
4. Compare provision language
5. Search media for implementation stories

**For Conflicts:**
1. Start with high-value legislation
2. Use eCitizen to search sponsor's companies
3. Check EACC annual reports
4. Search "[Sponsor Name] business interests" in media
5. Document even partial disclosures

## Success Metrics

**Quality over Quantity:**
- 3 well-documented cases > 10 poorly documented
- Complete metadata is essential
- Multiple sources verify confidence level
- Court judgments are primary sources

**Daily Target:**
- 1-2 court cases (high quality)
- OR 3-5 constitutional provisions
- OR 1 workaround (comprehensive)
- OR 2-3 conflict cases

**Weekly Review:**
- Are sources verified?
- Is metadata complete?
- Any duplicates?
- Gaps in documentation?
