# Chanuka - The Casual Pitch

## What is it?

Chanuka is a civic engagement platform that makes it stupid easy for Kenyans to track what's happening in Parliament. Think of it as "GitHub for democracy" - you can follow bills, see who's voting for what, join discussions, and actually understand what your representatives are doing.

## The Problem

Right now, if you want to know what's happening in Parliament, you're stuck reading dense legal documents, hunting through government websites, or relying on biased media coverage. Most people have no idea what bills are being passed or how they'll affect their lives until it's too late.

## What We Built

A modern web platform where you can:
- Track bills in real-time (like following a GitHub repo)
- Get AI-powered analysis that explains complex legislation in plain language
- See constitutional implications automatically flagged
- Join community discussions and vote on your stance
- Get notified when bills you care about move forward
- Switch between English and Swahili seamlessly

## The Tech

We're building this as a professional monorepo with React + Express + PostgreSQL. Everything's TypeScript, properly tested, and built to scale. We've got real-time notifications, ML-powered analysis, and we're working toward full WCAG AA accessibility.

## Current Status

Pre-launch, targeting Q2 2026. Core features work - bill tracking, user auth, community features, constitutional analysis. We're polishing accessibility, fixing TypeScript errors, and adding advanced features like electoral accountability tracking.

## Why It Matters

Democracy works better when people actually know what's happening. We're making legislative transparency accessible to everyone, not just lawyers and policy wonks.

---

**TL;DR:** We're building the platform that should've existed years ago - one that makes it easy for regular people to engage with their government and hold representatives accountable.
